CLINICAL NURSING AND OPERATIONS HIRING RUBRIC — INTERNAL USE ONLY

OVERVIEW
This rubric is used to assess candidates for clinical nursing roles and operations positions within the health system. Patient safety is the primary standard. All competency assessments should be grounded in specific behavioral evidence, not self-reported attributes.

CLINICAL COMPETENCY
Candidates must demonstrate current, accurate clinical knowledge appropriate to the role. Probe for specific protocols and procedures they have executed. Strong candidates can articulate not only what they did but why — including the clinical rationale. Watch for candidates who describe procedures at a surface level without demonstrating underlying clinical reasoning.

PATIENT SAFETY MINDSET
Every decision should default to patient safety. Ask candidates to describe situations where they identified a safety risk and what they did. Red flags include candidates who deferred to hierarchy without escalating clear safety concerns, minimized adverse events, or described workarounds to safety protocols as acceptable practice.

COMMUNICATION AND COLLABORATION
Clinical environments are interdisciplinary. Candidates should demonstrate effective communication with physicians, patients, families, and other staff under pressure. Assess for active listening, clarity under stress, and the ability to escalate concerns appropriately. Poor communicators create dangerous handoff environments.

EMOTIONAL RESILIENCE AND COMPOSURE
Clinical work involves loss, trauma, and sustained stress. Candidates should provide evidence of how they maintain composure and continue providing quality care under emotionally difficult circumstances. Distinguish between candidates who have developed healthy coping strategies versus those who suppress or avoid.

INTEGRITY AND ACCOUNTABILITY
Patient care requires accountability. Candidates should describe situations where they made an error and how they handled it — disclosure, correction, and learning. Candidates who claim never to have made a mistake or who deflect accountability should be noted.

SCORING ANCHOR NOTES
Score 1: Demonstrates safety risks or lacks minimum competency for the role
Score 3: Demonstrates adequate competency with some support; safe to practice with supervision
Score 5: Demonstrates advanced competency; contributes to team safety culture; models best practices

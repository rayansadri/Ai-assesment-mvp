MBA PROGRAM ADMISSIONS EVALUATION RUBRIC — INTERNAL USE ONLY

OVERVIEW
This rubric guides the evaluation of MBA applicants through interviews and application review. All readers and interviewers should apply criteria consistently and document specific evidence for each dimension.

ACADEMIC READINESS
Candidates should demonstrate quantitative proficiency sufficient for rigorous coursework. Prior academic performance is a signal but not deterministic — candidates with non-traditional backgrounds may show readiness through professional experience. Look for evidence of intellectual curiosity, ability to learn quickly, and comfort with ambiguity.

LEADERSHIP POTENTIAL
We are looking for candidates who have exercised real influence — not just titles. Strong candidates describe situations where they drove outcomes through others, navigated disagreement, and built something new. Distinguish between candidates who led and those who participated. Watch for use of "we" language without specifying their individual contribution.

PROGRAM FIT
Candidates should be able to articulate specific goals they want to achieve through the program and explain why this program in particular. Generic answers referencing "network" and "brand" without specificity are weak signals. Strong candidates have done their research and can describe how specific courses, faculty, or concentrations align with their goals.

DIVERSITY OF PERSPECTIVE
The cohort model requires candidates who bring distinct viewpoints. Assess professional background, geographic experience, industry context, and personal narrative for what they uniquely contribute to peer learning.

INTEGRITY AND AUTHENTICITY
Applicants sometimes misrepresent their roles or impact. Listen for specific details — authentic accounts contain granular information that is hard to fabricate. If a candidate's story changes between application materials and interview answers, note the discrepancy.

SCORING ANCHOR NOTES
Score 1: No evidence of the competency. Generic or rehearsed answers without specificity.
Score 3: Adequate evidence with some specificity. Minor gaps in reasoning or reflection.
Score 5: Compelling evidence. Specific, reflective, differentiated. Adds clear value to the cohort.
